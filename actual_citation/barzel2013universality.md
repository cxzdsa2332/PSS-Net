# barzel2013universality

- **Title:** Universality in network dynamics
- **Authors:** Baruch Barzel, Albert-László Barabási
- **Journal/Year:** Nature Physics, 9(10), 673–681, 2013
- **DOI:** 10.1038/nphys2741
- **Source consulted:** Europe PMC REST (DOI:10.1038/nphys2741); Semantic Scholar (DOI:10.1038/nphys2741)

## Verbatim opening (abstract)

"Despite significant advances in characterizing the structural properties of complex
networks, a mathematical framework that uncovers the universal properties of the
interplay between the topology and the dynamics of complex systems continues to elude
us."

## Remainder [summary — condensed by retrieval tool]

The paper presents a self-consistent theory that systematically separates the
contributions of network topology from those of the dynamical process. It derives
distinct universality classes from the governing continuum (rate) equations and
validates them across biochemical dynamics and epidemic-spreading models, including
real social and biological systems, without requiring a complete theoretical model.
