# wang2016databased

- **Title:** Data based identification and prediction of nonlinear and complex dynamical systems
- **Authors:** Wen-Xu Wang, Ying-Cheng Lai, Celso Grebogi
- **Journal/Year:** Physics Reports, 644, 1–76, 2016
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.physrep.2016.06.004
- **Source consulted:** Semantic Scholar (DOI:10.1016/j.physrep.2016.06.004)

## Verbatim opening (abstract)

"The problem of reconstructing nonlinear and complex dynamical systems from measured
data or time series is central to many scientific disciplines including physical,
biological, computer, and social sciences, as well as engineering and economics."

## Remainder [summary — condensed by retrieval tool]

A Physics Reports review of data-based reconstruction methods for nonlinear/complex
systems: compressive sensing for sparse-signal reconstruction, noise-induced dynamical
mapping, perturbation-based reverse engineering, synchronization, inner composition
alignment, global silencing, Granger causality, and optimization algorithms, drawing on
statistical/nonlinear-physics concepts (phase transitions, bifurcation, stability,
robustness), with the long-range goal of controlling complex systems.

## Supports in intro

B1 — authoritative review establishing that reconstructing the governing equations /
interaction structure of nonlinear complex systems from data is a central,
cross-disciplinary inverse problem, and that sparsity/perturbation are core tools —
directly relevant to PSS-Net's sparse, perturbation-based approach.
