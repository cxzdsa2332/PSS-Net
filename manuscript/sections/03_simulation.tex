\section{Simulation Studies}
\label{sec:simulation}

\subsection{Experimental setup}
\label{sec:sim:setup}
We evaluate PSS-Net on controlled nonlinear systems where the true directed
network is known.  The simulations are intended to probe two questions: whether
the perturbed steady-state regression recovers the support of sparse cross-node
effects, and whether the double-sparse estimator improves over a group-sparse
continuous-shrinkage baseline.

The primary additive benchmark follows the working model in
\Cref{sec:method:model}, with low-order polynomial effects and sparse directed
coupling.  In addition, we include a multiplicative generalized
Lotka--Volterra (gLV) benchmark as an example of a nonlinear system whose
positive steady states reduce to an additive linear equilibrium equation.  For
this benchmark,
\begin{equation}
  \frac{dx_j}{dt}
  =
  x_j
  \left(
  r_j + \sum_{i\neq j} A_{ji}x_i - \gamma_j x_j + u_j
  \right),
  \qquad j=1,\ldots,p,
  \label{eq:sim-glv}
\end{equation}
where $A_{ji}$ denotes cross-node coupling and $\gamma_j>0$ denotes
self-damping.  At any positive steady state, division by $x_j^*$ gives
\[
  r_j + \sum_{i\neq j} A_{ji}x_i^* - \gamma_j x_j^* + u_j = 0,
\]
so the same steady-state regression used by PSS-Net is correctly specified even
though the transient gLV dynamics are not additive.

For each simulated system, we generate $N$ perturbation conditions, integrate the
ODE to equilibrium, add measurement noise, and fit each target node separately.
The first perturbation is the unperturbed reference condition used for local
Jacobian evaluation.  Unless otherwise stated, the basis dictionary is the
no-intercept monomial basis used in \Cref{sec:method:basis}.  Network recovery is
summarized by TP/FP/FN/TN, precision, recall, $F_1$, and MCC.  Parameter recovery
is summarized by coefficient $\ell_2$ error and non-self Jacobian RMSE.  Results
are averaged over repeated random seeds and reported as mean $\pm$ standard
deviation.
